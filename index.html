Understanding the Basics

What Is a Broker?

Before you invest, you should fully understand where your money is held and who regulates it. Here is a clear and honest explanation.

A Broker Is to a Trader What
a Bank Is to a Customer

Just as a bank holds your money, processes your transactions, and is regulated by the Central Bank of Nigeria to protect your deposits — a licensed forex broker holds your trading capital, executes your trades, and is regulated by international financial authorities to protect your funds.

You do not send money to PPG. You deposit directly into your own personal account with our recommended licensed broker — a regulated financial institution. We are simply granted permission to trade on your account, just as you might authorise a trusted stockbroker to trade shares on your behalf.

Your money is segregated by law — kept entirely separately from the broker's own operational funds. This is a legal requirement of every regulated broker worldwide. Your capital cannot be touched for any other purpose.

📈 Why Higher % Is Harder Than It Looks

Making 1% consistently every day in forex is genuinely difficult — even professional traders work hard for it. It is the most realistic and sustainable target. As you move up the tiers, you are not just increasing potential gains — you are exponentially increasing risk and complexity. Choose honestly, not ambitiously.

⚖️ Risk vs Reward — The Real Trade-off

A 20% daily target requires much larger position sizes, dramatically increasing the chance of a significant losing day. Members on higher tiers must be fully prepared — emotionally and financially — to experience large swings, including potentially losing all deposited capital.
